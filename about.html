About Fendi

Founded in Rome in 1925, Fendi is an iconic luxury fashion house celebrated for its impeccable craftsmanship, innovative designs, and timeless elegance. From its origins as a family-run fur and leather shop, Fendi has evolved into a global symbol of Italian sophistication, blending tradition with modern creativity. Known for its signature Baguette bag, cutting-edge ready-to-wear collections, and exquisite accessories, Fendi continues to redefine style while honoring its rich heritage. Every piece is a testament to artistry, quality, and the bold spirit of Roman luxury. Fendi is an Italian luxury fashion house founded in Rome in 1925 by Adele and Edoardo Fendi. Originally starting as a fur and leather shop, it has since evolved into a global icon of style, sophistication, and craftsmanship. Known for its innovative designs, superior quality, and timeless elegance, Fendi offers a wide range of products including handbags, ready-to-wear clothing, shoes, leather goods, fragrances, and accessories. Signature Features
The Baguette Bag – A fashion icon since 1997, symbolizing effortless luxury. The FF Logo – Also known as “Zucca” or “FF” logo, designed by Karl Lagerfeld in 1965.
Innovative Craftsmanship – Blending traditional techniques with modern creativity. Heritage and Innovation Fendi’s philosophy embraces a deep respect for Italian craftsmanship, while continuously experimenting with bold creativity and modern trends. Karl Lagerfeld, who served as the creative director for over 50 years, played a key role in defining Fendi’s image, alongside Silvia Venturini Fendi. Global Presence Today, Fendi is part of the LVMH Group (Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton) and has boutiques in major fashion capitals worldwide, including Rome, Paris, New York, Tokyo, and Hong Kong.
